Quick Troubleshooting Tips (FAQ)

These simple checks can often resolve minor issues instantly, saving you time and the need for a service call. If these steps do not work, please contact us!

This is the most common fix for temporary glitches across almost all devices: **Power it Off and On Again.** For a computer, do a full shutdown, wait 30 seconds, and restart. For a modem or router, unplug it for 60 seconds and then plug it back in. This simple step often clears temporary memory errors and re-establishes network connections.

**Move Your Router:** Place your router in the center of your house, off the floor, and away from thick walls, metal objects (like refrigerators), and large mirrors. If your house is large, consider investing in a **Mesh Wi-Fi System** or **Wi-Fi Extender** to cover dead zones. We specialize in setting these up for maximum speed and coverage.

It's highly likely you have some form of **malware or adware**. **Do NOT click any pop-ups** claiming to fix your computer, as they are often part of the infection. Immediately disconnect from the internet (turn off Wi-Fi or unplug the cable) and run a full scan using any reputable antivirus software you have installed. If the problem persists, this is one of our flat-rate services‚Äîwe can remove the threat and install better protection.

**Implement a reliable backup strategy.** The most valuable thing on your computer is your personal data (photos, documents, etc.). We recommend the "3-2-1 rule": three copies of your data, on two different media types, with one copy off-site (cloud). We specialize in setting up an automated backup system, which is inexpensive insurance against hardware failure or cyberattack.

Quick Troubleshooting Tips (FAQ)

These simple checks can often resolve minor issues instantly, saving you time and the need for a service call. If these steps do not work, please contact us!

This is the most common fix for temporary glitches across almost all electronic and mechanical products: Perform a power cycle. This clears temporary software errors without affecting your settings.

  1. Turn Off: Completely turn off the device using the main power button or switch.
  2. Unplug: Locate the power cord and unplug the device from the wall outlet or power strip.
  3. Wait: Wait for at least 30 seconds. This ensures all residual power drains from the internal components, allowing a proper internal reset.
  4. Plug In: Plug the device back into the power source.
  5. Turn On: Turn the device back on and test the function that was having issues.

If a single function fails, the cause is often a loose connection or a minor obstruction.

Check Physical Connections

  • Wiggle and Secure: Gently wiggle and firmly re-seat all connected cables (power, data, audio, etc.) into their respective ports on both the device and the wall or auxiliary equipment.
  • Look for Damage: Check the cable ends and ports for any visible damage, bent pins, or foreign debris.

Check for Obstructions

  • Clear the Path: For mechanical devices (like printers, vacuums, or automatic feeders), physically inspect the area where the error is occurring and clear any small debris or foreign objects.
  • Clean Vents: Ensure all air vents and cooling fans are free of dust buildup to prevent overheating, which can cause intermittent performance issues.
